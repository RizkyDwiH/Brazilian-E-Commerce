# Brazilian E-Commerce Customer Behavior Analysis

## Project Overview
This project analyzes customer behavior patterns from Olist Brazilian E-commerce dataset to identify business opportunities and provide data-driven recommendations for improving customer retention and revenue growth.

## Business Problem
Olist is facing a critical customer retention challenge where 94% of customers make only one purchase despite high satisfaction ratings, indicating a significant opportunity for loyalty program implementation and customer experience improvements.


## Tools & Technologies
- **SQL Server** - Data analysis and querying
- **Tableau** - Data visualization
- **Git** - Version control

## Key Findings

### 1. Customer Retention Crisis
- **94.24%** of customers make only one purchase
- Only **5.76%** return for repeat purchases
- High satisfaction scores (**4.15/5**) suggest retention issues are not product-related

### 2. Geographic Revenue Concentration
- **São Paulo** generates **37%** of total revenue ($509M)
- Top 3 states (SP, RJ, MG) account for **62%** of total revenue
- Clear geographic focus for marketing efforts

### 3. Product Category Performance
- **Beauty & Health** leads with **$124M** revenue and **4.19/5** rating
- **Watches & Gifts** follows with **$117M** revenue
- All top categories maintain **4.0+** satisfaction scores

## Business Recommendations

### Immediate Actions:
1. **Implement Loyalty Program** - Target 94% one-time buyers with incentives
2. **Launch Email Marketing Campaigns** - Reactivate satisfied one-time customers
3. **Focus on São Paulo Market** - Allocate resources to highest-revenue region
4. **Expand Beauty & Health Category** - Leverage top-performing product line

### Strategic Initiatives:
- Develop customer segmentation for personalized marketing
- Create win-back campaigns for inactive customers
- Optimize product recommendations based on purchase history
